SABAE, FUKUI PREFECTURE
MADE IN JAPAN · EST. 1967
"The hand that shapes the frame is the same hand that carries generations of craft."

One artisan.
One frame.
No shortcuts.

Each Matsuda frame is the work of a single artisan from the famous Sabae region of Fukui Prefecture — home to over 90% of Japan's eyewear production. The process involves more than 200 individual steps, from initial acetate cutting to final hand-polishing.

Matsuda's design language draws on Japan's cultural heritage — from classical woodblock print patterns to Art Nouveau sculptural forms — reimagined for the contemporary luxury consumer.
